Cricket is not merely bat and ball,
It is a rhythm, a heartbeat, a call.
From dusty streets to stadium lights,
It lives in days and sleepless nights.

The morning sun begins to rise,
Hope and passion fill the skies.
A child with bat in hand stands tall,
Dreaming of cheers beyond the wall.

The pitch lies calm, a silent stage,
Ready to witness skill and rage.
Eleven warriors take their place,
Determination etched on every face.

The bowler runs with measured fire,
Eyes locked with fierce desire.
Each step carries pressure and pace,
A battle of mind, of strength, of grace.

The batter waits with steady sight,
Prepared to turn defense to might.
One perfect ball can break the game,
One fearless shot can spark a flame.

The leather swings beneath the air,
Crowds erupt in hope and prayer.
An edge, a miss, a cracking drive,
In every moment, dreams survive.

A cover drive through the field so clean,
A sight so pure, almost serene.
The ball races fast across the grass,
And cheers explode as boundaries pass.

Then comes the spinner, clever and sly,
Making the red ball dance and fly.
Flight and drift, deception deep,
Even legends lose their sleep.

A wicket falls — silence near,
Then thunderous roars fill every ear.
Hands in air, teammates unite,
One breakthrough can change the fight.

Cricket teaches patience too,
Not every plan will see you through.
Sometimes you block, sometimes attack,
Sometimes life pushes you back.

Test cricket whispers ancient art,
Demanding skill, discipline, heart.
Five long days of mental war,
Where grit decides who legends are.

ODIs bring balance, calm and speed,
Every over shapes the need.
Build the innings, time the chase,
Pressure grows with every phase.

T20 storms in like blazing fire,
Pure adrenaline, raw desire.
Sixes flying into the night,
Crowds dancing under stadium light.

Yet cricket’s beauty isn’t fame,
Or records written beside a name.
It’s in the friendships that survive,
In every game that feels alive.

In gullies where young dreamers play,
Using broken bats at end of day.
A brick for stumps, a tennis ball,
Still cricket means the world to all.

It teaches courage after defeat,
To rise again and compete.
A duck today, a hundred tomorrow,
Joy can follow deepest sorrow.

Captains plan with thoughtful mind,
Searching weaknesses they can find.
Field placements, tactics, subtle art,
Leadership tested from the start.

Last over now, the tension grows,
Nobody knows how the story goes.
Six runs needed, one ball left,
Hope and fear together cleft.

The bowler breathes, the batter waits,
Destiny stands beside the gates.
Ball is bowled — the bat swings free,
Time slows for all humanity.

Up it goes into the night,
Thousands stare at the flight.
Will it clear or will hands claim?
One moment can define a game.

That is cricket — cruel yet kind,
A sport of body, soul, and mind.
More than numbers on a chart,
It leaves a mark inside the heart.

For once cricket enters your soul,
It becomes a lifelong role.
Through every cheer and every scar,
It reminds us who we are.

So raise the bat, shine the ball,
Respect the game that gives its all.
For cricket is not just a sport to see—
It is emotion, passion, poetry.
